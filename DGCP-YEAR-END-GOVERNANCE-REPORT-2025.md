# DGCP Year-End Governance Report 2025

**Organization:** MaMeeFarm™  
**Framework:** DGCP™ (Data Governance & Continuous Proof)  
**Reporting Period:** 18–30 December 2025  
**Report Date:** 30 December 2025  
**Status:** Official Public Governance Report

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This Year-End Governance Report documents the completion of the 2025 governance framework for MaMeeFarm™ DGCP™.

Between 18 and 30 December 2025, MaMeeFarm™ established a complete, auditable, and ethically governed system for transforming real-world work into verifiable data.

The framework prioritizes cryptographic truth, human dignity, and long-term resilience over platform dependency or narrative authority.

---

## 2. Governance Scope & Coverage

As of year-end 2025, DGCP™ governance covers:

- Evidence integrity and time authority
- Platform non-reliance and narrative boundaries
- Proof invalidation and non-destructive revocation
- Data ownership, custody, and AI usage controls
- Economic rights and ethical monetization
- Risk, threat, and adversarial resistance models
- Ethics oversight, appeals, and dispute resolution
- Metrics, KPIs, self-audit, and transparency standards
- Privacy preservation, anonymization, and safety-by-design
- Data lifecycle, retention, federation, and partnerships
- Governance maturity, succession, crisis response, and continuity

All governance documents are publicly indexed and version-controlled.

---

## 3. Key Governance Milestones (Dec 18–30, 2025)

- **Dec 18:** Core governance principles established (time authority, platform non-reliance)
- **Dec 19–22:** Enforcement, ownership, AI control, and economic boundaries defined
- **Dec 23–24:** Risk models, ethics oversight, appeals, and standards alignment introduced
- **Dec 25–27:** Maturity levels, metrics, audits, data quality, and public trust frameworks completed
- **Dec 28–29:** Privacy, safety, lifecycle, federation, and ecosystem governance finalized
- **Dec 30:** Master index, operational playbook, and governance closure published

This sequence forms a complete and coherent governance architecture.

---

## 4. Risk, Ethics, and Public Trust Posture

DGCP™ adopts a proactive governance stance:

- Risks are identified before escalation
- Adversarial scenarios are anticipated
- Ethical oversight guides enforcement without altering cryptographic truth
- Appeals and dispute resolution preserve fairness and accountability
- Public trust is grounded in verifiable evidence, not narrative persuasion
