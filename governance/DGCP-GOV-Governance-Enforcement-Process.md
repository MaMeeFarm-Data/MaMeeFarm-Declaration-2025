# DGCP Governance: Enforcement Process

**Effective Date:** 20 December 2025  
**Status:** Active Governance Policy  
**Applies to:** All DGCP™ Governance Violations and Disputes

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines a structured, auditable process for enforcing DGCP™ governance policies.

---

## 2. Enforcement Triggers

Governance enforcement may be initiated by:

- License violations
- Unauthorized AI usage
- Proof misuse or misrepresentation
- Platform dependency violations
- Breach of custody or ownership rules

---

## 3. Enforcement Actions

Depending on severity, actions may include:

- Public governance notice
- Proof invalidation or revocation
- License suspension or termination
- Registry updates and annotations

---

## 4. Due Process & Transparency

All enforcement actions must be:

- Documented
- Timestamped
- Publicly auditable where appropriate
- Non-destructive to historical records

---

## 5. Governance Integrity

Enforcement prioritizes:
- System trust
- Proportional response
- Long-term auditability
- Protection of real-work data contributors
