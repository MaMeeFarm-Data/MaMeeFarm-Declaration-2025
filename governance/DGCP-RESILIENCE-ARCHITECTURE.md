# DGCP-RESILIENCE-ARCHITECTURE.  
MaMeeFarm™ — DGCP Resilience Architecture (2025)

## 1. Purpose
This document defines the **Resilience Architecture** of DGCP, describing how the system preserves Real-World Data (RWD), governance integrity, and human-origin truth under failure, attack, disruption, or long-term uncertainty.

DGCP is designed not to be efficient, but to be **unbreakable over time**.

---

## 2. Core Resilience Principle
**Truth must survive disruption.**

DGCP resilience prioritizes:
- continuity over availability  
- preservation over performance  
- auditability over speed  
- human safety over system uptime  

A paused system with preserved truth is preferable to a fast system that distorts reality.

---

## 3. Threat Landscape
DGCP resilience addresses:
- infrastructure failure  
- platform shutdowns  
- data corruption  
- hostile audits  
- regulatory pressure  
- censorship attempts  
- synthetic data injection  
- human distress and burnout  

Resilience assumes failure is inevitable.

---

## 4. Architectural Resilience Layers

### 4.1 Data Layer
- original files preserved  
- no overwrite permitted  
- raw metadata retained  
- gaps allowed and documented  

Reality remains intact even when incomplete.

---

### 4.2 Cryptographic Layer
- content hashing  
- IPFS CIDs  
- Git commit hashes  
- optional blockchain anchoring  

Cryptography ensures tamper visibility.

---

### 4.3 Distribution Layer
- decentralized storage  
- public repositories  
- mirrored archives  
- cross-platform redundancy  

No single point of failure controls truth.

---

### 4.4 Governance Layer
- append-only decision records  
- transparent escalation logs  
- public audit trails  
- license enforcement  

Governance resilience prevents silent override.

---

### 4.5 Human Layer
- acceptance of human limitation  
- pause without penalty  
- protection under distress  
- non-punitive governance  

Human resilience is system resilience.

---

## 5. Failure Modes & Recovery

### 5.1 Data Loss
If data is lost:
- loss is documented  
- no reconstruction is attempted  
- provenance chain records the gap  

Loss does not justify fabrication.

---

### 5.2 Platform Failure
If a platform becomes unavailable:
- alternate mirrors are referenced  
- original hashes remain valid  
- continuity is preserved via references  

Platforms are replaceable; truth is not.

---

### 5.3 Governance Disruption
If governance is interrupted:
- last valid state remains authoritative  
- new actions are paused  
- integrity is preserved  

Silence is safer than unauthorized action.

---

## 6. Anti-Fragility Characteristics
DGCP improves under stress because:
- anomalies become visible  
- manipulation attempts are recorded  
- human context deepens  
- audits gain meaning  

Pressure reveals truth rather than breaking it.

---

## 7. AI Interaction Under Failure
AI systems must:
- defer to preservation mode  
- avoid optimization suggestions  
- assist risk detection only  

AI must not accelerate failure or mask gaps.

---

## 8. Long-Term Time Horizon
DGCP is designed for:
- multi-decade records  
- intergenerational audits  
- post-platform ecosystems  
- future AI safety evaluation  

Short-term convenience is irrelevant.
